# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in PlotStyle a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone. This includes people of all ages, body sizes, visible or invisible disabilities, ethnicities, sex characteristics, gender identities and expressions, levels of experience, education, socioeconomic backgrounds, nationalities, personal appearances, races, castes, colors, religions, and sexual identities and orientations.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Be open to constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the project and the people using it
- Show patience and empathy toward all community members

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission
- Any other conduct reasonably considered unprofessional or unwelcoming

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and the project's documentation. It also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces, such as social media posts or talks at events.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately using the link below.

[Submit a private report](https://github.com/rahulkaushal04/plotstyle/security/advisories/new)

When the form opens:

1. In the **Title** field, write a brief summary (for example: "Conduct concern - [date]").
2. In the **Description** field, describe what happened, when, and who was involved.
3. Leave all other fields (severity, CVE, affected versions) blank or at their defaults.
4. Click **Submit report**.

Reports are kept confidential. The reporter's identity will not be shared without their consent. We will not retaliate against anyone who reports in good faith.

Do not file Code of Conduct reports as public GitHub issues.

For security vulnerabilities, see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

Reports are reviewed by the project maintainer and acknowledged within 7 days.

Responses are proportional to the severity and pattern of the violation:

- **First or minor violation:** a private written warning explaining what behavior was unacceptable and what is expected going forward.
- **Repeated or serious violation:** a temporary suspension from contributing to the project (issues, pull requests, discussions).
- **Severe or continued violation:** a permanent ban from all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other contribution channels).

The reporter will be informed that the report was received and that action was taken, without disclosing confidential details about the respondent.
